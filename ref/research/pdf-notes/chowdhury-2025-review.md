## 0. Headline for the world-builder

Chowdhury et al. is a **timing + mechanism** synthesis, not a hypsometry-number
paper — but Figures 1, 6, and 7 carry the quantitative spine, and one of its
mechanism claims **contradicts a load-bearing number in our survey** (ocean-basin
depth; see §"Survey corrections"). Their synthesis position:

- Early Archean (>3.2 Ga) Earth was **largely a water world**, with emergence
  **transient** and exposing **precratonized volcanic crust** (plateaus / ocean
  islands) for **<10–20 Myr** at a time [P, p.458, p.464].
- **First stable, craton-scale land: ca. 3.2–3.0 Ga**, by **isostatic rise of
  magmatically thickened & felsic-differentiated cratonic crust** over a thick
  buoyant mantle keel — driven by **magmatism, not compression** [P, p.459, p.466].
- Emergent land **area grew 3.0 → 2.5 Ga** as more cratons formed + **plate
  tectonics began** [P, abstract, p.460].
- **Culminated ca. 2.5–2.2 Ga**, land area + freeboard "resembled today's" [P,
  abstract/p.444].

This **matches our survey §1 arc closely** — the fetch-read got the narrative
right. The corrections below are about *quantities and mechanisms*, not the timeline.

## 1. Modern reference numbers (their Introduction, §1) — [P]

Useful because they confirm/correct our survey's "modern Earth" column **and** one
`ASSUMPTIONS.md` entry.

- Continental crust: **~40 km thick, felsic**, "typically occurs above sea level";
  less dense *partly* by composition **but mostly by its exorbitant thickness**
  (Lee et al. 2018) [P, p.446]. Modern reference value **~41 km** [F, Fig 6b caption].
- Oceanic crust: **~7 km thick, mafic**, "predominantly subaqueous" [P, p.446].
- Mean seafloor water depth: **~3.8 km** [P, p.446].
- **~29%** of Earth's surface = emergent crust **+ submerged continental shelves**;
  the rest is ocean basins [P, p.446]. (Note: their 29% *bundles* shelves with
  emergent land — subaerial-only is lower; our project's measured 29.2% land is
  comparable-but-defined-differently.)
- Ocean basins contain **~1.34 billion km³ of water** (Eakins & Sharman 2010) [P,
  p.446]. → **Verifies/corrects `ASSUMPTIONS.md`** "planetary water inventory"
  row, which had "≈1.39×10⁹ km³ *from memory, verify*." The literature value as
  cited here is **1.34×10⁹ km³**, not 1.39.
- Continents "mostly hover just above sea level (within 1 km) with low-lying
  topography"; only mountain belts (Himalayas) are high; a small fraction (shelves)
  is below sea level [P, p.446].
- Continental **freeboard near-constant within ±200 m** of modern mean sea level
  **through the Phanerozoic and perhaps the Proterozoic** (Wise 1974, Galer 1991)
  [P, p.446]. Note the bound: this is a **Phanerozoic/Proterozoic** claim — the
  Archean is exactly where it breaks. Matches survey §5.
- Mantle δ¹⁸O reference range **5.3 ± 0.6‰** [P, p.454].
- TTG diagnostic composition (sidebar, p.450): **K/Na < 0.6–0.8, SiO₂ > 64 wt%,
  La/Yb > 15, Sr/Y > 15** [P].

## 5. Emergence mechanisms they credit (candidate uplift-nomos designs) — [P]

The paper's central contribution is **distinct mechanisms before vs after the
Paleo–Mesoarchean (~3.2 Ga) boundary** [P, abstract]. Three regimes, in time order:

### M1. >3.2 Ga — magmatic plateau thickening + collapse (TRANSIENT emergence)
- Mantle upwelling → **decompressive melting** → thick **mafic-ultramafic submarine
  plateau crust ~20–35 km** (MgO ~18 to >20 wt%; from mantle melt fractions ≤10%)
  [P, sidebar p.463; §3.4.1 p.464].
- Successive magmatic pulses + coeval TTG magmatism thicken the plateau to
  **~35–40 km**; **magmatic inflation + dynamic lithospheric uplift** push it
  transiently above the seafloor [P, p.464].
- **Self-limiting:** when magmatism wanes, the edifice **collapses** and magmatic
  inflation subsides → **resubmergence**. Cyclic, ~10–20 Myr per cycle (Fig 7c) [P].
- Buoyancy source = **thick low-density mafic plateau crust** riding high (reduced
  density contrast with ambient submarine crust), NOT felsic composition [P, p.464].
- Analogues named: Phanerozoic oceanic plateaus (**Shatsky Rise, Ontong Java,
  Caribbean**) and ocean islands (**Hawaii, Iceland**) [P, p.458/464].
- **Plus meteorite-impact uplift**: bolide bombardment generated **km-scale
  topography** (spherule layers, Kaapvaal/Pilbara; Barberton Mendon Fm example)
  [P, p.464–466].
> **Nomos sketch:** episodic magmatic-thickening source term with a **decay/collapse**
> term; isostatic buoyancy from thick-but-mafic crust; output = *transient,
> low-amplitude, self-erasing* emergent platforms. Good model for a Phase-2/early-
> Abyssal "flickering islands on a water world" look.

### M2. ~3.2–3.0 Ga — cratonization + isostatic rise (FIRST STABLE land)
- Crustal **thickening from ~35 km → ~50–55 km** + intracrustal differentiation
  → **mature, layered, FELSIC (TTG→K-granitoid) cratonic crust** (less dense) [P,
  §3.4.2 p.466].
- Growth of a **thick (>150 km), cold, melt-depleted, buoyant mantle keel (CLM)**
  — melt depletion lowers its density and water content, raising buoyancy [P, p.457].
- **Isostatic adjustment** of (thicker + less-dense crust + buoyant keel) →
  **stable subaerial elevation ~1–1.5 km** [P, p.466]. **Driven by magmatic
  processes, not compressional tectonics** [P, p.466].
- Hard cap: **significant topography (>2 km) unlikely** for Archean cratonic
  plateaus, "due to their reduced strength under hotter geotherms" (Rey & Houseman
  2006, Rey & Coltice 2008) [P, p.466].
> **Nomos sketch:** an **isostasy** response to three coupled inputs — (a) crustal
> thickness↑, (b) crustal density↓ (felsic differentiation), (c) mantle-keel
> buoyancy. *Permanent* (keel resists recycling) vs M1's transient. This is the
> "first real land" event; freeboard ~1–1.5 km, capped <2 km.

### M3. ~3.0–2.5 Ga — plate-tectonic compressional thickening (ELEVATED relief)
- **Secular mantle cooling** → rigid lithospheres able to transmit stress → **plate
  tectonics onset** → compressional deformation at cratonic margins thickens **both
  crust AND lithospheric mantle** → **high-standing orogenic belts, high subaerial
  relief** (Kublik et al. 2024) [P, §3.4.3 p.466–467].
- Concurrent driver: **"gradual deepening of oceanic basins and the concomitant
  thickening of continental lithospheres"** (Korenaga 2017), both "ultimately linked
  to the secular cooling of the mantle" [P, p.467].
> **Nomos sketch:** the *modern* orogen mechanism (collisional crustal thickening)
> **+ a secular ocean-basin-deepening term** that raises freeboard globally as the
> mantle cools. This is where relief >2 km and modern bimodality finally appear.

### The master variable: SECULAR MANTLE COOLING — [I]
All three regimes are downstream of one monotonic clock. Cooling drives: oceanic
crust **thinning** (basins deepen), continental crust **thickening + felsic
differentiation** (cratons rise), **keel growth** (buoyancy), and the **tectonic-
mode transition** (plateau magmatism → plate tectonics). **A single "mantle
temperature" parameter (a global time-clock) could drive an entire emergence
nomos** — this is the cleanest single-lever backbone the paper offers for a
world-builder. [I, synthesized from §3.3–3.4]

### The isostasy equation they give (directly implementable) — [P]
"Continental Freeboard" sidebar (p.446), verbatim:

> ρ_sw(b + d_r) + ρ_oc·b_oc + ρ_olm·b_olm + ρ_am·b_am = ρ_sw·b + ρ_cc·b_cc + ρ_clm·b_clm

ρ_sw = seawater density; b = sea level relative to modern value; d_r = mean depth
of mid-ocean ridge; (oc) oceanic crust, (olm) oceanic lithospheric mantle, (am)
asthenospheric mantle, (cc) continental crust, (clm) continental lithospheric
mantle; isostatic compensation at the base of the continental lithospheric mantle
(detailed model: Korenaga et al. 2017). **"All parameters (except ρ_sw) likely
differed on early Earth compared to today and had evolved over time during the
Hadean and Archean Eons"** [P]. This is a concrete freeboard/uplift relation an
isostasy nomos could implement directly.

## 6. SURVEY CORRECTIONS (`early-continents-survey.md`)

Ordered by how load-bearing the correction is.

### 6.1 ⚠ Ocean-basin depth — the survey's number is contradicted by this review
- **Survey §5/§6 target:** *"Mean ocean depth ~5–6 km (deeper than modern; ~50%+
  more water)."* Tagged the "deeper basins" as the sharpest correction to the
  placeholder.
- **Chowdhury's synthesis:** early Archean seafloor was **SHALLOW, not deep** — a
  hotter mantle made **thick (~20–35 km), MgO-rich, buoyant mafic submarine
  crust** that "**led to a shallow Archean seafloor**" [P, p.461]; ocean **basins
  DEEPENED over time** as the mantle cooled and oceanic crust thinned ("increasing
  depth of ocean basins," Fig 1b; "gradual deepening of oceanic basins," p.467) [P].
- **What's actually right in the survey:** the **more-water** half. Chowdhury's
  reference model assumes **50% more voluminous Archean oceans** [P, Fig 6b caption]
  and says the shallow seafloor was *"potentially holding substantially larger
  volumes of ocean water than those observed today"* [P, p.461].
- **The reconciliation** [I]: the water world came from **more water + shallow
  basins + low/drowned continents**, NOT from deep basins. Thick buoyant oceanic
  crust raised the seafloor; the extra water then spilled over the low continents.
  So the survey **conflated "more water volume" with "deeper basins"** — the first
  is supported, the second is *reversed* by this review.
- **Engineering upshot:** the placeholder's oceans (~0.3 km, "12× too shallow")
  are still wrong for every era — the *direction* (much deeper than 0.3 km) holds.
  But the **specific target "~5–6 km, deeper than modern" is not supported here**
  and is likely too deep for the early/mid Archean. A defensible early-Earth basin
  is **around modern (~3.8 km) or somewhat less**, with the water-world look
  produced by **low continental freeboard + more water**, not ultra-deep abyss.
  (Fig 6d plots a ~4–5 km "range of oceanic depths" at 3.2–3.0 Ga [F], so "much
  shallower than modern" would overcorrect — the honest read is *comparable to
  modern, with the lever being low land + extra water*, not basin depth.)
  **Recommend rewriting survey §6's "mean ocean depth" row** to: *early basins
  ~modern-or-shallower (thick buoyant Archean oceanic crust), deepening with time;
  water-world driven by low freeboard + ~50% more water, not deep basins.*

### 6.2 Early/mid-Archean LAND stood only ~1–1.5 km, not "3–5 km peaks"
- **Survey §3/§6:** "Max relief ~3–5 km" (from the Sci. Adv. densification paper),
  presented as the landform constraint.
- **Chowdhury:** emerged cratons at 3.2–3.0 Ga had **subaerial elevation ~1–1.5 km**
  [P, p.466]; **>2 km topography unlikely** pre-late-Archean [P, p.466]; craton-
  emergence **peak relief ~1 km** [P, Fig 6d]. Higher relief (orogenic belts)
  only **after ~3.0 Ga with plate tectonics** [P, §3.4.3].
- **Not a contradiction — a different quantity.** The survey's 3–5 km is
  *peak-to-trough total relief* (includes basin depth); Chowdhury's ~1–1.5 km is
  *subaerial freeboard* (how high land stands above sea level). **For a
  world-builder the operative "how high is the land" number for the pre-late-Archean
  is ~1–1.5 km, capped <2 km** — the survey should add this distinction so the
  reader doesn't build 3–5 km early *mountains*. [I]

### 6.3 Land fraction — Chowdhury pins the *timeline* the survey left as a band
- **Survey §6:** "Emergent land 2–15% (central 3–10%)" for pre-2.5 Ga, big spread.
- **Chowdhury** sharpens it *in time* [P/F, Fig 6b]: **<few % before ~3.2 Ga**
  (only transient volcanic islands/plateaus) → **>10% by ~3.0 Ga** → **~30% by
  ~2.5 Ga** → modern ~29%. For a **Phase-2 / early-Abyssal (pre-emergence,
  mostly-submerged)** world the target is the **low end — a few percent** — which
  is well inside the survey's band. The survey's band is fine; Chowdhury just
  makes the *rise* steep and late (steep climb 3.2→2.5 Ga).

### 6.4 Things the survey got RIGHT (confirmed against the pages)
- **Timeline** (transient/volcanic pre-3.2 → stable cratons 3.2–3.0 → growth
  3.0–2.5 → modern-like 2.5–2.2): **confirmed** [P, abstract + §3.2].
- **Crustal-growth debate: bracket, don't average** — **confirmed as the right
  posture.** Chowdhury runs freeboard models under **both** rapid (Armstrong) and
  slower (Campbell/Roberts–Spencer) growth curves and **picks no winner** [P, Fig
  6b]; even notes rapid growth *worsens* the water world (more continental volume
  displaces seawater over continents) [P, p.462]. Survey §4/§7 "do not average" holds.
- **Water-world adjudication:** early Earth largely a water world but **not zero
  land** (transient volcanic emergence persists) — **matches** survey §7's
  "reject literal total submergence, accept very-little-mostly-transient-volcanic"
  [P, abstract/p.458].
- **Freeboard low/negative early, modern-like by 2.5–2.2 Ga, ±200 m constant only
  since Phanerozoic/Proterozoic** — **matches** survey §5 [P, p.446].
- **Continental crust ~30–50 km, needs ~45–55 km to emerge** — **confirmed**:
  cratons thickened ~35→50–55 km and emerged at that thickness [P, §3.4.2];
  crustal-thickness peak ~55–58 km at ~3.0 Ga [F, Fig 6c].
- **Oceanic (submarine) crust ~25–35 km** — **confirmed** (Chowdhury: ~20–35 km,
  MgO ~18 to >20 wt%) [P, sidebar p.463]. Survey's 25–35 km is inside this.
- **Compressed/muted early bimodality developing toward modern sharp bimodality**
  — **confirmed** by Fig 1b's curve sequence [P/F].

### 6.5 Small factual fixes
- Transient emergence episode duration: **<10–15 Myr** (§3.2.1, p.458) / **~10–20
  Myr** (Fig 7c) [P] — a concrete number the survey didn't have.
- `ASSUMPTIONS.md` ocean-water inventory: literature value **1.34×10⁹ km³**
  (Eakins & Sharman 2010, per this review), not the "1.39 from memory" placeholder.

**Shape targets** (robust even where km values are soft):
1. **Hypsometry evolves, doesn't sit static.** A faithful early world is one *frame*
   of Fig 1b — pick an age and read its curve. Phase-2/early-Abyssal = the **">3.5
   Ga water world"** frame: a **tiny mafic-plateau hump near/just-below sea level +
   a shallow-amplitude basin**, water over almost everything.
2. **The water world = low continents + more water, NOT a deep abyss.** Get the
   *land* low (freeboard ≤0 to ~+1 km) and the *water inventory* generous; do
   **not** reach for 5–6 km basins.
3. **Emergence is isostatic and time-progressive.** If the world advances through
   the Abyssal phase, land should *rise* via M1→M2→M3, and **ocean basins should
   deepen in step** — both driven off one secular-cooling clock.

**Mechanism sketches for an uplift nomos** (detail in §5): **M1** episodic
magmatic-plateau thickening + collapse (transient islands); **M2** isostatic
cratonic rise from crustal thickening + felsic densification-decrease + keel
buoyancy (first stable land, ~1–1.5 km); **M3** compressional/plate-tectonic
thickening + secular basin-deepening (elevated relief, modern bimodality). The
**isostasy equation** in §5 is directly implementable; the **single-parameter
mantle-temperature clock** [I] is the cleanest backbone.

## 8. Scope notes
- Skipped per brief: the biosphere/oxygenation/climate implications (§4) and the
  detailed geochemical-proxy timing arguments (§2.2: Sr, Zn, O isotopes, K/La) are
  read but only *timing*-relevant — they corroborate the 3.2–2.5 Ga emergence
  window (multi-proxy change at 3.2–2.5 Ga, Fig 3/5), not hypsometry numbers. One
  quantitative proxy datum worth keeping: subaerial volcanism rose from **<20% to
