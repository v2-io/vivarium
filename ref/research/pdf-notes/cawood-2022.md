## 0. Headline findings (the five that matter for vivarium)

1. **The seven-phase scheme is a named, dated skeleton that maps almost 1:1 onto doc/PHASES.md**
   — and it is explicitly a *tectonic-mode* progression driven by monotonic mantle cooling.
   Proto → Primordial → Primitive → Juvenile → Youthful → Middle → Contemporary, with
   tectonic sub-modes magma-ocean → heat-pipe → squishy-lid → transitional → early-plate →
   modern-plate (Fig 1 p3, Fig 17 p32). [PR]

2. **Bimodal hypsometry is a *derived* late feature, and Cawood's mechanism is crust-side,
   not ocean-side.** The whole of §8 (pp35–37) argues bimodality was *impeded* early
   because early mafic crust (~20–30 km) and the thin/mafic proto-continental crust had too
   little thickness/density contrast; it "started to become prominent" only once thick
   (~40–50 km) mature felsic cratons existed **by ca. 3.2–3.0 Ga**. The early "water world"
   is achieved by **absence of emergent land**, not by deeper oceans. [PR] This is the
   single most load-bearing correction to the survey (see §5).

3. **The generation-vs-preservation framing the survey adopted is not just supported — Cawood
   endorses the preservation side outright.** p13: preserved Archean-age crust is **<15%**
   while the volume *present at that time* may have been **>75%** of today's; "This
   discrepancy requires extensive recycling of continental lithosphere." The paper concludes
   the continental record is a **secondary/preservational** archive, biased by the
   supercontinent cycle — not a primary/generational one. [PR]

4. **A crustal-thickness-through-time trajectory the survey lacked**: TTG melt-depth proxies
   give global crust ~**40 km at 3.8 Ga → ~55 km at 3.2 Ga**, Singhbhum ~**33→48 km
   (3.5→3.2 Ga)**, then a slight decline ~**55→50 km (3.2→2.5 Ga)**; cratons mature and thick
   (>40–45 km) only since ~3.2 Ga (pp21, 23, 36). [PR] Emergence tracks this: thick felsic
   crust floats isostatically above the mafic ambient once it passes ~40–45 km.

5. **Freeboard was NOT monotonic to a modern plateau.** Cawood adds a **mid-Proterozoic
   ("boring billion", 1.8–0.8 Ga) freeboard DIP** — thin orogenic crust → lower elevations →
   lower weathering/Sr flux — bracketed by a Nuna-assembly high (~1.8 Ga) and a Neoproterozoic
   rise, "constant subsequently" (p37). The survey's story stops at "modern-like by 2.5–2.2
   Ga" and misses this non-monotonicity. [PR] (Not load-bearing for vivarium's Abyssal-phase
   world, which is pre-2.5 Ga, but recorded.)

are the paper's own; **evidence-grade** is the paper's own hedging, restated. Cawood is
emphatic (p27) that the tectonic-mode assignment is disputed across **~85% of Earth history
(Hadean→Neoproterozoic)** — so every pre-2.5 Ga assignment is model-dependent and the grades
below are *within the paper's framing*, not consensus.

**The three-mode compression (the paper's own simplification, p30 / p38):** everything
reduces to (a) **stagnant-lid**, (b) **sluggish/squishy-lid** (distributed deformation), (c)
**active-lid = plate tectonics** (narrow deformation). Figure 15 (p28) is the canonical
schematic; the right-hand panels are surface-velocity profiles (flat for stagnant; broad
gradients for squishy; sharp step-changes at narrow boundaries for plate tectonics).

**Load-bearing quotes for the timeline:**
- p39: *"Only in the period 3.2–2.5 Ga did the transformation into a rigid whole lithosphere
  occur, enabling the development of a globally linked system of plate boundaries with the
  Earth operating under an active lid, plate tectonic mode since at least 2.5 Ga."*
- p34: *"the transition to plate tectonics at a global scale happened over a protracted time
  period and most likely, was not completed until ca. 2.5 Ga."*
- p27: *"differing interpretations of tectonic mode for when plate tectonics initiated,
  ranging over ~85% of Earth history from the Hadean to the Neoproterozoic. This level of
  uncertainty for the fundamental process controlling the Earth system is remarkable."*

## 2. Hypsometry, freeboard, emergence — the numbers

### Modern reference (Figure 4, p5; ETOPO1 / Amante & Eakins 2009) [PR]
- Highest point Mt Everest **8849 m**; deepest Mariana Trench **−10911 m**.
- **Avg land elevation 797 m** (survey uses ~840 m — see §5); **avg ocean depth 3686 m**
  (survey ~3.7 km — matches).
- Bimodal; continental-lithosphere break at **~40% cumulative area** on the hypsographic curve.
- p2: continental crust "includes the bulk of the emerged crust as well as the submerged
  continental shelves … **~40% (~210 × 10⁶ km²) of the planet's surface area** but **70% of
  the total volume** of oceanic and continental crust (~7,182 × 10⁶ km³)." **[Note: 40% is
  continental-*crust* incl. drowned shelves, NOT emergent land — see §5.1.]**

### The §8 emergence / bimodality story (pp35–37) [PR]
- p36: *"early mafic crust (prevalent during the Hadean-Archean) was considerably thicker
  (~20 to 30 km) than the modern oceanic crust."*
- p36: TTG genesis requires *"crustal thicknesses of at least 25–35 km."*
- p36: *"The blurring of the difference between the thickness of the early mafic crust and the
  continental crust must have impeded the development of bimodal hypsometry."*
- p36: *"the cratonic crust started becoming stable, compositionally mature, and thick (>40–45
  km) only since ca. 3.2 Ga."*
- p36: *"during its early history, the Earth may have been a water world with no significant
  amount of stable, emergent landmasses (except for some volcanic islands)."*
- p36: *"Once the stabilized cratons with thick (~40 to 50 km) mature crust came into
  existence by ca. 3.2–3.0 Ga … the bimodal hypsometry started to become prominent."*
- p36: emergence "need not have been controlled by plate tectonic processes. Instead, the
  formation of thick, felsic crust bearing cratons … at non-plate tectonic settings imparted
  them a significant isostatic uplift … and eventually pushed them above the sea level, leading
  to the appearance of stable, continental landmasses **by ca. 3.2–3.0 Ga**."
- p36: *"a significant increase in the proportion of emergent continental landmasses by the end
  of Archean to early Paleoproterozoic."*
- p37 (freeboard non-monotonicity): Nuna amalgamation "tectonically accentuated the continental
  crust thickness … facilitated continental uplift and a **high freeboard**"; then "The middle
  Proterozoic is inferred to be characterized by **thin, orogenic continental crust** … which
  led to **lower continental elevations** above sea level"; Neoproterozoic "re-appearance of
  orogenic mountains … an increase in emergent landmass"; "The bimodal hypsometry and the
  continental freeboard seem to have remained **constant subsequently** through modern plate
  tectonics."

### Other-planet framing [PR p35]
Moon, Mars, Venus have **unimodal** hypsometry (single mafic-dominated crustal population).
Mars is bimodal (thin young northern lowlands vs thick old southern highlands) *but not from
plate tectonics*. Cawood: modern-Earth bimodality (40:60 continental:oceanic) "is a direct
outcome of plate tectonics." [INF: this is the survey's "bimodality is a plate-tectonic
fingerprint" claim, page-read-confirmed.]

## 3. Crustal / lithospheric structure through time (geophysics core)

- **Continental crust**: avg thickness ~35–40 km, range <20 km (hyperextended margins) to >80
  km (Himalaya-type collision); ~0.5% of Earth's mass but 100× enriched in U/Th/K → ~⅓ of
  radiogenic heat (pp2,7). [PR]
- **Oceanic crust**: 6–7 km, up to ~40 km at oceanic plateaus, ~0 at core complexes (p2). [PR]
- **Early mafic crust**: ~20–30 km (§8, p36); pre-2.5 Ga oceanic-type crust "likely thicker
  (>20 km)" and coeval continental crust "more mafic" (p5). [PR]
- **Crustal-thickness trajectory (Archean)** [PR pp21,23,36]:
  - Singhbhum TTG genesis depth ~33 km @3.5 Ga → ~48 km @3.2 Ga.
  - Global Eu/Eu* melt-depth ~40 km @3.8 Ga → ~55 km @3.2 Ga (Tang 2021).
  - 3.2–2.5 Ga: slight decrease, ~55→50 km (Tang) OR ~40→35 km (Balica) — model-dependent.
- **Continental lithospheric mantle**: few-10s km (hyperextended) to ~200 km (Precambrian
  cratons); chemically-defined max ~200 km (xenolith), thermal ~250 km; some active regions
  (Tibet/Iran) to 260 km (pp8,11). Fig 6 (p7): Archean lithosphere 200–250 km thick, oceanic
  lithosphere up to 100 km. [PR]
- **Crust:lithospheric-mantle thickness ratio**: ~1 at modern convergent margins, as small as
  **0.1** at stable Archean cratons (p8). [PR]
- **Surface heat flux**: Archean cratons 30–40 mW/m²; Proterozoic & younger 60–80 mW/m² (p7).
  [PR]
- **Cratons**: up to ~60% of present continental lithosphere; "strong surfaces … maintained
  positions at or near sea-level for long periods" (unconformable flat sub-horizontal covers
  back to late Archean) (pp10–11). [PR]
- **Upper-crust composition secular change** [PR p7]: upper-crust MgO ~15% @3.2 Ga → modern-like
  ~4% by 2.6 Ga (Tang 2016); ~70% of Archean continental crust was relatively mafic (bulk MgO up
  to ~10% vs ~5% post-Archean); present bulk continental crust ~43% mafic, concentrated in lower
  crust.

## 4. Crustal-growth-curve controversy (Figure 10a, p13) — how Cawood renders it

Figure 10a plots **volume of continental crust (%) vs age** for ~a dozen models spanning the
two end-members [PR]:
- **Early-formation / near-100%-early, ~flat**: Fyfe (1978), Armstrong (1981). (These are the
  "generation front-loaded, near-steady-state via recycling" camp.)
- **Gradual net growth**: Goodwin (1996), Allègre & Rousseau (1984), Condie & Aster (2010),
  Dhuime (2012), Belousova (2010), Condie (2003), Pujol (2013), Roberts & Spencer (2014),
  Artemieva (2006), plus the present-day surface-age distribution (dashed).

**Cawood's own position is NOT a neutral average** — this is the key nuance vs the survey's
"bracket, don't average" instruction (which Cawood *satisfies*, but from one side):
- p13: preserved Archean-age crust **<15%**; volume present at that time **>75%** of today's;
  "This discrepancy requires extensive recycling of continental lithosphere, and at times this
  must have been equal to, or greater than, the rate of continental generation."
- p13: current recycling may exceed generation by ~1 km³/a since at least the Mesozoic → a ~15%
  decrease in continental volume since the mid-Neoproterozoic.
- §5–§6 (pp13–17): the paper repeatedly argues the frequency record (zircon age peaks, passive
  margins, metamorphic ages) is a **secondary/preservational** signal tied to the supercontinent
  cycle, NOT a primary/generational one — siding with Cawood/Hawkesworth, against
  Parman/Condie's primary-signal reading.

**[INF]** So Cawood is best cited as: (i) confirming the survey's "generation was front-loaded,
preservation was back-loaded" framing at [PR] grade and with sharper numbers (>75% present vs
<15% preserved for the Archean), and (ii) explicitly endorsing the *preservation-side*
interpretation of the record — the survey should stop presenting the two camps as symmetric
where Cawood is cited, and note Cawood picks a side.

- **Mantle potential temperature** (Fig 10b, p13): models peak ~1500–1600 °C around 2.5–3.5 Ga,
  declining to ~1350 °C today; Urey ratio Ur(0)=0.23 reference line. This monotonic cooling is
  the paper's *first-order control* on tectonic-mode change (p32). [PR]

## 5. Survey corrections (`early-continents-survey.md`)

Ordered by load-bearing weight.

### 5.1 — HIGH: the survey's "deepen the oceans to 5–6 km" fix gets NO support from Cawood
The survey (§5/§6, and ASSUMPTIONS.md) makes *shallow oceans* "the load-bearing error" and
prescribes ~5–6 km mean ocean depth (leaning on Korenaga 2021 + Dong 2021). **Cawood's
water-world is a *low-land* water world, not a deep-ocean one.** §8 explains early submergence
entirely by lack of emergent land (thin/mafic crust not standing above sea level) and is
**silent on any secular increase in ocean volume/depth** — Cawood neither endorses nor cites
the deeper-early-oceans mechanism in the geophysics sections. [PR pp35–37; INF for the "silent"
claim — I read §8 in full and it does not appear.] **Action:** the survey should flag that its
highest-priority quantitative fix rests on Korenaga/Dong *alone*; this broad review does not
corroborate it. The direction "early Earth was submerged" is robust and shared; the *reason*
(deep oceans vs low land) is not — and the most authoritative review here takes the low-land
route. For vivarium this matters: an early-Earth look is achievable via **low emergent land over
a normal-depth ocean**, which is cheaper and better-supported than re-deepening the basins.

### 5.2 — MED-HIGH: early mafic/oceanic crust thickness is ~20–30 km, not 25–35 km
Survey §2/§6 headlines "~25–35 km" (with a footnote that some estimates are ~20 km, tagged
uncertain). Cawood's repeated central figure is **~20–30 km** (p36, citing Herzberg 2010,
Korenaga 2013, Sleep & Windley 1982), with ">20 km" as the floor for >2.5 Ga oceanic-type crust
(p5). [PR] **Action:** shift the survey headline to ~**20–30 km central (20–35 km range)** and
promote the low-end note; the survey's own uncertainty flag is vindicated by the review's
central value.

### 5.3 — MED: Chowdhury 2025 and Cawood 2022 are not independent
The survey treats Chowdhury, Cawood & Mulder (2025) and Cawood et al. (2022) as separate
corroborating anchors for emergence timing (3.2–3.0 Ga stable, later global). They share
**Cawood and Mulder as co-authors** (and Chowdhury). Their agreement is one research group's
consistent position, not independent convergence. [INF from author lists.] **Action:** note the
shared authorship; Flament (2008) remains the genuinely independent anchor.

### 5.4 — MED: add the crustal-thickness-through-time trajectory
The survey gives a static "~30–50 km continental, needs ~45–50 km to emerge" but no time-path.
Cawood supplies one (§3): ~40 km @3.8 Ga → ~55 km @3.2 Ga globally; emergence follows once crust
matures past ~40–45 km (~3.2–3.0 Ga). [PR] **Action:** add this as the mechanistic link between
"crust thickens" and "land emerges" — it's exactly the uplift-nomos driver vivarium wants.

### 5.5 — LOW-MED: modern avg land elevation
Survey uses ~840 m; Cawood's Fig 4 (ETOPO1) reads **797 m**. [PR] Minor; prefer 797 m if citing
this figure. Also: keep the survey's **29% emergent land** distinct from Cawood's **40%
continental crust** (the latter includes drowned shelves) — these are different quantities and
must not be conflated.

### 5.6 — NEUTRAL/CONFIRM: relief cap
Cawood is *directionally* consistent with the survey's "~3–5 km max relief, no Himalayas early"
(hot weak crust can't support high loads; thin mid-Proterozoic crust → low elevations; pp36–37)
but gives **no competing quantitative peak-relief bound**. [PR] **Action:** keep the survey's
"~3–5 km [High]" anchored on the Sci. Adv. densification paper; cite Cawood as directional
mechanism support only, not as a second number.

### 5.7 — CONFIRM (strengthen): generation-vs-preservation and bimodality-is-plate-tectonic
Both survey framings are page-read-confirmed and can be up-tiered where they cite Cawood: the
<15%-preserved / >75%-present Archean figure (§4 above) and the unimodal-other-planets /
bimodality-as-plate-tectonic-fingerprint claim (§2 above).

## 6. World-builder extraction

Material for doc/PHASES.md phase/sub-phase structure and for uplift / tectonic *nomoi*. Grades are
**evidence grades for the real-Earth claim** (per Cawood's hedging), separate from this note's
[PR]/[INF] source-fidelity tags.

### 6.1 Phase/regime timeline (drop-in for doc/PHASES.md cross-check)
Vivarium's **Abyssal (Phase 3)** — "a mostly-submerged early world whose land emerges *during*
the phase" — maps precisely onto Cawood's **Primitive→Juvenile (3.8–2.5 Ga)** window:
- **Phase-3 opening state** ≙ Primitive Earth: squishy-lid tectonics, granite-greenstone +
  TTG crust ~40 km, **only transient/local emergence** (shallow-marine platformal sediments),
  no stable land. Grade: MED. This is the "water world with volcanic islands" backdrop.
- **Phase-3 emergence event** ≙ Juvenile Earth 3.2–3.0 Ga: thick (>40–45 km) felsic cratons
  reach isostatic uplift and **push above sea level → stable continental landmasses appear**,
  bimodal hypsometry becomes prominent. Grade: MED. **This is the mechanism vivarium wants land
  to emerge *from* — crustal thickening/maturation, not sea-level drop.** [PR pp23,36]
- **Phase-3 → Phase-4 charge**: emergence + subaerial weathering + widespread mafic volcanism
  drives CO₂ drawdown and the O₂ whiffs that build toward the GOE (late-Archean onset) — i.e.
  Abyssal's photosynthesizers + the *newly emergent, weatherable land* together manufacture the
  oxygenation key. Cawood ties GOE onset to "subaerial exposure of the cratons and widespread
  mafic magmatism" (p25). Grade: MED. [PR] This corroborates doc/PHASES.md's charge-chain logic.

### 6.3 Later-phase mining (Phases 5–8 will want these)
- **Youthful/Middle/Contemporary uplift & orogeny**: supercontinent cycle (Nuna ~1.8, Rodinia,
  Pangea) drives crustal-thickening pulses → freeboard/relief oscillations; "early (hot) → modern
  (cold)" plate-tectonic shift at ~0.8 Ga enables deep subduction, UHP metamorphism, tall
  collisional mountains. [PR pp35,37,39]
- **Orogen taxonomy** (Fig 8, p10) for any future mountain-building nomos: collisional
  (Alpine-Himalayan), accretionary (Cordillera), intracontinental (Tian Shan) — classified by
  whether converging lithosphere is thick-buoyant or thin-dense. Collisional fold-belts are a
  *plate-tectonic* landform → scarce/absent before ~2.5 Ga (consistent with survey §3). [PR]
- **Basins** (Fig 9, p12): oldest large passive margins + foreland basins ~3 Ga → a lateral-
  motion (mobility) marker; cratonic basins are slow-subsiding intracratonic sag basins with
  flat marine fill. [PR]
