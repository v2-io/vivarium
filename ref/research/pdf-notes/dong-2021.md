## 0. The one-line answer

Dong et al. is a **volume/inventory** paper, not a depth paper. Its result:
**early Archean (4–3.2 Ga) surface oceans were a *lower bound* of ~1.0–1.4
ocean-masses if the present mantle holds ~1 ocean-mass of water — i.e. "somewhat
bigger than today, by a modest and strongly present-mantle-water-dependent
amount," not a confident 50%+.** The mechanism produces its "bigger ocean" as
**flooding of the continents** (a water world), *not* as deeper abyssal basins.
That distinction is the load-bearing correction to how our survey cites this
paper. **[P]** for the numbers; the flooding-vs-depth framing is **[P]** on the
flooding side, **[I]** on the "not deeper basins" inference (see §5).

---

## 1. What the paper actually measures (and in what units)

The paper never reports a mean ocean depth. Its three outputs are:

1. **Bulk mantle water *storage capacity*** as a function of mantle potential
   temperature Tp (the max water the solid mantle can hold), in **ocean masses
   (OM)**. **1 OM ≡ 1.335 × 10²¹ kg H₂O** (their definition, from ETOPO1 /
   Eakins & Sharman 2010). **[P, Table 1 footnote]**
2. **Minimum early-ocean *volume*** (also in OM), derived by assuming the early
   mantle was water-saturated and the ocean+mantle budget was conserved. **[P]**
3. **Minimum flooded fraction of early continents** (%), from an isostatic
   model. **[P]**

Present-day ocean = 1 OM by construction. Their own isostatic model reproduces
the present ocean as 1.45 × 10²¹ kg, agreeing with the ETOPO1 value
(1.335 × 10²¹ kg) to **<12%**. **[P, p.7]**

**Unit bridge for the world-builder [I]:** 1 OM = 1.335 × 10⁹ km³. As a
global-equivalent layer (GEL) over the whole 5.1 × 10⁸ km² Earth: **1 OM ≈ 2.6
km GEL** (matches the ~2.7 km in `ASSUMPTIONS.md`, which uses the slightly larger
1.39 × 10⁹ km³ standard ocean volume).

## 2. Headline result — mantle water storage capacity vs temperature

**Table 1 (Bulk Water Storage Capacity in Earth's Solid Mantle, OM)** — read
directly **[P]**:

| Tp (K) | 5th %ile | 25th | **Median** | 75th | 95th |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1550 | 2.37 | 2.62 | **2.86** | 3.30 | 5.36 |
| **1600 (present)** | 1.86 | 2.07 | **2.29** | 2.65 | 4.41 |
| 1700 | 1.19 | 1.35 | **1.52** | 1.79 | 3.05 |
| 1800 | 0.83 | 0.96 | **1.10** | 1.32 | 2.32 |
| **1900 (early Archean)** | 0.52 | 0.62 | **0.72** | 0.90 | 1.69 |
| 1950 | 0.41 | 0.50 | **0.59** | 0.74 | 1.47 |

- Present mantle (Tp = 1600 K): storage capacity **1.86–4.41 OM, median 2.29**.
  **[P, Abstract + Table 1]**
- Early Archean (Tp = 1900 K, +300 K): **0.52–1.69 OM, median 0.72**. **[P]**
- The drop for a +200–300 K hotter early mantle is
  **1.19₋₀.₁₆⁺⁰·⁹⁰ – 1.56₋₀.₂₂⁺¹·¹⁶ OM**. **[P, Abstract + §3.3]**
- **The negative temperature dependence is the robust core**, "very robust
  because the uncertainties at different Tp are highly correlated," even though
  absolute capacities carry large error. **[P, p.10–11]** Physical driver: water
  storage in nominally anhydrous minerals (olivine/wadsleyite/ringwoodite/
  bridgmanite) *falls* with temperature. Bridgmanite (>half the mantle mass) is
  the dominant uncertainty. **[P]**

**Independent check on present mantle water content** (needed to turn capacity
into an ocean-volume prediction): geochemical H₂O/Ce estimates span **0.5–2 OM**;
the most rigorous (Hirschmann 2018) gives **0.55–0.95 OM**, at the low end.
Upper bound "likely does not exceed 2 OM"; **<0.5 OM would contradict** observed
deep water-enriched reservoirs. **[P, p.13]**

## 3. The early-ocean-volume result (the number our survey wants)

From **§3.4 + Figure 5** — early-ocean *minimum* volume as a function of
present-day mantle water content, at Tp = 1900 K **[P]**:

| Present-day mantle water | Min. early-Archean ocean (OM) | IQR |
|---|---|---|
| 0.5 OM | **0.77₋₀.₇₇⁺⁰·²⁰** (smaller than today) | 0.60–0.88 |
| 1 OM | **1.27₋₀.₉₆⁺⁰·²⁰** | 1.10–1.38 |
| 2 OM | **2.27₋₀.₉₆⁺⁰·²⁰** | 2.10–2.38 |

**The crossover:** early oceans were *bigger* than today **iff the present-day
mantle holds > 0.3–0.8 OM of water** (and early Tp ≥ 1900 K). **[P, Abstract +
§3.4 + Conclusions]** Since the best present-mantle estimate (0.55–0.95 OM) sits
just above that threshold, "somewhat bigger" is the defensible central call;
"much bigger / double" needs the high (≈2 OM) mantle-water assumption.

**These are explicitly *lower bounds***: the model assumes (a) the early mantle
was water-saturated and (b) the ocean+mantle total budget was constant. Both are
conservative — real early oceans could be larger (e.g. if water was later lost
to space, or the early mantle was undersaturated). **[P, p.13]**

## 4. The flooding result — this is where "water world" comes from

**§3.5 + Table 2 + Figure 6.** With continents at 100% of present size and height
(fc = fi = 1), Tp = 1900 K **[P, Table 2]**:

| Present-day mantle water | Median flooded fraction of early continents |
|---|---|
| 0.5 OM | 54% (IQR 19–74%) |
| **1 OM** | **92% (IQR 89–94%)** |
| 2 OM | 99% |

- "Most of the model results suggest a largely flooded early Archean Earth …
  consistent with a global surface ocean with little or no land (a 'water
  world')." **[P, p.16]**
- Early continents were more flooded than today (present ~27.5% submerged) iff
  present mantle water **> 0.4₋₀.₂⁺⁰·⁵ OM** (fc = fi = 1). **[P, p.14]**
- If early oceans were **>2× present volume** (Tp ≥ 1900 K, high mantle water),
  "the continents were possibly covered entirely by water, with only a small
  number of subaerial landmasses protruding … due to hot spot volcanism and/or
  oceanic plateaus." **[P, p.14]** — matches the survey's "isolated volcanic
  islands" landform inventory.

## 5. Depth vs. flooding — the load-bearing correction

**Dong's extra water floods continents; it does not (in this model) deepen the
abyss.** Reasoning:

- The paper's outputs are volume, flooded-%, and sea-level `h`. **No mean ocean
  depth or abyssal depth is ever tabulated.** **[P — confirmed across whole
  paper]**
- The bathymetry model (Appendix C, from Korenaga et al. 2017): a hotter mantle
  gives **thicker oceanic crust and shallower ridge-axis depth d₀(Tp)** — i.e.
  the early ocean *floor rode higher* — combined with faster subsidence b(Tp).
  **[P, Appendix C, Eqs C3–C4]** The "higher ocean floor" limb is exactly the
  survey §2 mechanism for a *compressed* (not deepened) early hypsometry.
- **[I] arithmetic:** a 1.3 OM inventory (≈ 1.74 × 10⁹ km³) spread over an early
  ocean covering ~90% of the surface (4.6 × 10⁸ km²) gives a mean depth of
  **~3.8 km — essentially modern** (modern: 1 OM over 71% = 3.7 km). The extra
  water buys **area (flooding), not depth**. To reach ~5–6 km mean depth from
  Dong you would need ~2 OM held *over a non-expanding ocean area*, which is not
  what the flooding model does.

**So the two "more-water" papers our survey co-anchors predict different
geometries:**
- **Korenaga (2021):** deeper basins, **~5–6 km mean depth**, ~1 km/Gyr
  shallowing since. Water lives in *deep basins*.
- **Dong (2021):** ~modern-depth ocean **spread over a flooded, near-global
  surface** (water world). Water lives in *flooded area*.

They agree on **direction** (more early surface water) and roughly on **timing**
(emergence/crossover late Archean), but disagree on **where the water goes**.
This matters for a world-builder: "deepen the basins to 5–6 km" (Korenaga) and
"keep basins ~modern depth but drown almost all the land" (Dong) build
visibly different planets.

## 6. Model assumptions that drive the result

1. **Bridgmanite water storage capacity** is the single dominant uncertainty
   (>half the mantle mass); its temperature dependence is imported via the
   ringwoodite→bridgmanite partition coefficient D = 15 ± 8 (Inoue et al. 2010),
   backed by Hernández et al. 2013 ab initio (~15). **[P, §2.2, §3.2]**
2. **Water-saturated early mantle + constant ocean+mantle budget** → the ocean
   numbers are **lower bounds**. **[P]**
3. **Early Archean Tp = 1800–2000 K (ΔTp = +200 to +300 K)** — the paper adopts
   this from multiple thermal-history studies; notes the range reflects
   "conflicting interpretations of petrological constraints." **[P, §3.3]**
4. **Isostatic/flooding model assumes plate tectonics** with two crustal
   densities (oceanic ρ = ρ(Tp), continental ρcc = 2890 kg/m³, hcc = 34 km,
   compensation at 200 km, buoyancy correction Δρ = −5 kg/m³). **[P, §2.5]**
5. Present-day hypsometry fit from **ETOPO1** (27.5% of continents submerged
   today); early hypsometry allowed a weaker-lithosphere vertical compression
   (Rey & Coltice 2008). **[P, §2.5]**

**Authors' own stated caveats [P, §3.3, §3.4, p.15]:**
- Lower-mantle NAM storage (bridgmanite) at P > 30 GPa is **not experimentally
  constrained**; pressure effect could revise the upper bound upward.
- Water storage is **not strictly additive** for complex rocks — pyrolite likely
  stores *less* than the mineral sum, so their capacities may be **upper bounds**
  on the storage profile.
- Slab hydrous phases add **≤ 0.3 OM** — small, and only widens the early-vs-
  modern gap.
- **Major flooding-model caveat:** if the early Earth had a *stagnant lid /
  single-density lithosphere* (Moore & Webb 2013) rather than plate tectonics,
  positive topography is inhibited and a **smaller** ocean would flood the same
  fraction — i.e. their ocean-volume inference is contingent on early plate
  tectonics.

## 7. The counter-position they engage (constant-ocean-volume tradition)

The paper is explicitly framed *against* the "long-held assumption that the
surface oceans' volume remained nearly constant through geologic time." **[P,
Abstract + §1]** The tradition rests on **continental-freeboard constancy**
(approximately constant through the Phanerozoic; Miller et al. 2005).

Their engagement is symmetric and honest **[P, Table 3 + Conclusions]**:
- **Table 3** inverts the question: for *constant freeboard* to hold at Tp =
  1900 K (fc = fi = 1), present-day mantle water must be **≤ 0.4₋₀.₂⁺⁰·⁵ OM
  (median)**. Since best estimates are 0.5–0.95 OM, constant freeboard is
  **disfavored** → early oceans were larger.
- **But the honest reverse:** "if excessively flooded early continents and deeper
  oceans can be excluded through an improved understanding of the geologic record
  … then the present-day mantle water content would have to be < 0.3–0.8 OM." So
  the debate is not closed — it turns on present mantle water content, which is
  itself uncertain to a factor of ~4 (0.5–2 OM).

**Relation to Korenaga:** Dong *uses* Korenaga et al. (2017) for the isostatic
and bathymetry parameterization and cites Korenaga heavily; the two are
co-travelers in the "more early water" camp, reaching it by **independent
mechanisms** (Dong: mantle storage-capacity ceiling falls when hot; Korenaga:
degassing/regassing history). Their agreement is real but not independent
confirmation of the *magnitude*.

## 8. Survey corrections (§5 / §6 of `early-continents-survey.md`)

1. **§5 Dong bullet — upgrade tier and sharpen.** Current text is [A] and reads
   "temperature-dependent mantle storage → larger early ocean; ocean shrinking
   as Earth cooled." That's directionally correct **[now P]**, but add: the
   magnitude is a **lower bound of ~1.0–1.4 OM for present-mantle ~1 OM (IQR
   1.10–1.38), only ~20–40% more than today — not 50%+** — and it is
   **conditional** (bigger-than-today only if present mantle water > 0.3–0.8 OM).

2. **§6 target-table row "Mean ocean depth ~5–6 km (~50%+ more water) — Source
   anchor: Korenaga 2021; Dong 2021" — remove Dong as an anchor for the depth,
   and soften the "50%+" it anchors.** Dong supports neither cleanly:
   - Dong reports **no mean depth**; its geometry is ~modern depth over a flooded
     surface (§5 above). The **~5–6 km mean depth is Korenaga-specific.**
   - Dong's central "more water" is **~20–40% more (conditional)**, not 50%+.
   Recommended rewrite: keep 5–6 km anchored to **Korenaga 2021 only**; add a
   note that **Dong 2021 argues the extra early water manifests as continental
   flooding at ~modern basin depth, not as a deeper abyss** — the two papers
   agree on direction, differ on geometry.

3. **§6 hypsometry-shape row.** Dong reinforces the "compressed / mostly-
   submerged" call from the *water-side*: hotter mantle → thicker oceanic crust
   → higher ocean floor → **compressed** vertical gap, plus continents drowned.
   Consistent with the current row; can cite Dong 2021 as a second anchor there
   (where it belongs) instead of on the depth row.

4. **§7 "live debate" paragraph.** Dong makes the debate sharper and more
   quantitative than the survey states: the disagreement reduces to **present-day
   mantle water content** (0.5–2 OM), with a clean pivot at ~0.4 OM (Table 3).
   Worth recording that the "constant-ocean" tradition is *not refuted*, only
   *disfavored under the best present-mantle estimate*.

## 9. World-builder extraction — what inventory to declare

Vivarium's planned pipeline (declare a water inventory → fill the hypsometry →
*derive* sea level) is **natively Dong's model** — Dong literally declares an
ocean volume, isostatically fills a hypsometry, and reads off flooding + sea
level. So Dong is the right primary anchor for the **inventory declaration**,
and Korenaga is the right anchor for the **basin-depth / hypsometry shape** if
the deep-abyssal look is wanted. These are **two independent levers**; the survey
conflated them.

**Recommended declared early-Abyssal water inventory:**

| | Value | GEL / notes | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Central declare** | **≈ 1.3 OM** (1.10–1.38 IQR) | 1.74 × 10⁹ km³; ~3.4 km GEL; ~30% more water than modern | **Med** — the "present mantle ~1 OM" case, near the best geochem estimate |
| **Honest low** | ~0.8 OM | slightly *less* than modern; the "present mantle 0.5 OM" branch | present-mantle-water-dependent |
| **Honest high** | ~2.3 OM | 3.0 × 10⁹ km³; ~5.9 km GEL; near-total flooding | needs present mantle ≈ 2 OM |
| **Direction** | bigger than modern | robust *iff* present mantle water > 0.3–0.8 OM | **Med-High** on direction, **Low-Med** on magnitude |

**How the declared inventory becomes a depth (the fork the survey missed):**
- If vivarium keeps a **modern-ish hypsometry** and declares ~1.3 OM, the ocean
  **floods a large fraction of the continents** and the *mean* depth stays
  **~modern (~3.7 km)** — this is the **Dong** planet (drowned, low-freeboard,
  ~modern-depth). **[I]**
- If vivarium *also* wants the **~5–6 km deep basins** the survey targets, that is
  a **separate hypsometry change** (deepen the abyssal population), anchored to
  **Korenaga, not Dong**. Declaring more water alone will not deepen the abyss —
  it will drown more land. **[I]**
- Either way, **the placeholder's ~12×-too-shallow ocean is wrong** — Dong
  confirms the direction (more water, drowned world), just via flooding rather
  than depth.

**Concrete suggestion:** declare inventory **1.3 OM (range 0.8–2.3 OM honest
spread)** for an early-Abyssal world, *and separately* choose whether the
hypsometry gives deep basins (Korenaga geometry) or a flooded shallow-abyss
water world (Dong geometry). If forced to one number: **1.3 OM**, tagged
`literature`, sourced Dong 2021 (inventory) + Korenaga 2021 (if deep-basin
geometry is chosen).

## 10. Load-bearing quotes (verbatim [P])

- Abstract: *"more voluminous surface oceans might have existed if the actual
  mantle water content today is > 0.3–0.8 OM and the early Archean Tp was
  ≥1900 K."*
- Plain-language summary: *"water storage capacity in a hot, early mantle may
  have been smaller than the amount of water Earth's mantle currently holds, so
  the additional water in the mantle today would have resided on the surface of
  the early Earth and formed bigger oceans. Our results suggest that the
  long-held assumption that the surface oceans' volume remained nearly constant
  through geologic time may need to be reassessed."*
- §3.4: *"If the mantle today holds more water than its water storage capacity
  during the early Archean … more voluminous surface oceans must have existed in
  the past."*
- §3.5 / p.16: *"Most of the model results suggest a largely flooded early
  Archean Earth … consistent with a global surface ocean with little or no land
  (a 'water world')."*
- Conclusions: *"if in the future an improved understanding of the early Archean
  geological record rules out the possibility of larger oceans, then the
  present-day mantle water content is constrained to be < 0.3–0.8 OM, and likely
  much less than this range."*
