## ⚖ The discretisation foundation — the fork everything waits on, and four probes that could convict us

*Source: [`doc/theory/discretisation-and-information.md`](doc/theory/discretisation-and-information.md) (2026-07-13), out of the Cardiff & Demirdžić FV review + the information-theoretic reading. **This is the "principles first, tactics after" work Joseph asked for** — the existing kernels are knowingly ad-hoc and are NOT the point; the point is that a new agent authoring a nomos knows what it is claiming. `DECISIONS[algorithms-are-disguised-physical-claims]`, `[column-is-a-control-volume-with-sufficient-statistics]`, `[bias-vs-noise-is-the-decisive-audit]`.*

### ⛔ THE FORK — decide the column semantics

**Is a stored scalar a POINT SAMPLE, a CELL AVERAGE, or a BAND-LIMITED sample? Declared, per quantity.** Today the code answers **three different ways** — `mean-pin` assumes a cell average, `voxel.rs`'s bilinear assumes a point sample, and `gen.rs`'s `nyquist_level` produces a band-limited sample. In information terms these are **three different anti-aliasing filters**, and the disagreement is not cosmetic: **only the cell-average branch makes volumetric exactness available at all** (a centroid-linear reconstruction is volume-exact *by construction*; an interpolant through point values is off by a **curvature bias** that cancels globally and is wrong in every local budget). **Every mesh, seam, and conservation question is unanswerable until this is decided.**

### The four probes (cheap, falsifiable, each could convict us)

1. **Null-space / eigenvalue probe** — assemble each field nomos's operator on a periodic patch and **count zero eigenvalues**. Every one beyond the rigid-body modes is a mode the kernel **is blind to**, and its eigenvector shows what it looks like (Cardiff §3.5.3, Fig. 17). *This is the only instrument we have that reveals what we cannot see.* ⚠ Suspicion: `water.rs`'s θ flux-smoothing is **Rhie–Chow-class stabilisation** — an unphysical term suppressing exactly such a mode (the "travelling soliton blobs"), and **its physical claim is NONE**. `ASSUMPTIONS.md` should say so.
2. **Curl probe** — gravity-driven flow is a **gradient flow**, so it is **curl-free identically**. Does MFD's biased fan produce a flow field with **nonzero discrete curl**? If yes we are making **water circulate on hillsides** — a *violated identity*, not an accuracy problem, and no refinement fixes it.
3. **Well-balanced probe** — does a flat lake over a bumpy bed stay **dead calm at $t=0$**, or does the scheme generate spurious currents that friction then eats? Those are very different claims and **only one is a scheme property**. (We have lakes and a "flat lakes emerge" test — but emerging-by-dissipation ≠ well-balanced.)
4. **Fluctuation–dissipation probe** — `water.rs` adds θ-dissipation and **no matching fluctuation**. Mori–Zwanzig says they are a **pair**. Prediction (unverified): systematic sub-grid energy loss — **a bias**. Measure it.

### Then build

5. **Carry a sufficient statistic, not a mean** — at minimum a **sub-grid variance**, with an **exactness** contract. Jensen's inequality makes this **non-optional** ($\overline{K A^m S^n} \neq K \bar A^m \bar S^n$; for $n>1$ we *systematically underestimate erosion, forever*), and fluctuation–dissipation makes it **the thing that sets the jitter amplitude** — so the jitter stops being a knob and becomes a *derived quantity*. This is the deferred spike-2 `:statistic`/`:exactness` contract, now load-bearing.
6. **⚠ SPIKE: the multiresolution (wavelet) store** — highest-leverage item here. If coarse = low-pass of fine, then **fine = coarse + detail**; store the *details* and the coarse↔fine relation becomes an **identity instead of a constraint**. **Mean-pin IS the Haar low-pass with the detail coefficients thrown away — which is precisely why refluxing is needed.** Store them and *the seam never happens*. It may also **retire the "one hard research problem"** (`detail→abstract`): in a multiresolution store the coarse coefficient is a **linear functional** of the fine ones, so an agent edit propagates up the pyramid as a delta in $O(\log N)$, exactly. **Hypothesis. Spike it; do not build on it.**
7. **Reconstruction per consumer, declared** — flux wants centroid-linear + **discontinuous** (volume-exact); rendering wants continuous $C^1$; drainage wants **monotone** (no spurious pits — otherwise Priority-Flood spends compute solving a problem *our own mesh invented*). **They must differ**, and their discrepancy is bounded and known. This is the core/view wall, one level deeper.
8. **Powers-of-two is open per phenomenon** — dyadic refinement is *information-uniform* for **scale-free** (fractal) fields, so it is principled for the fBm prior *for real reasons*. It is **arbitrary for any process with a characteristic scale** (channel width, convective cell, grain size, canopy), and vivarium will be full of those.

**The measurements (nine grids, `examples/grid_lab/` → `ref/research/grid-comparison-report.md`; reproducible, Euler asserted at construction so a topology bug cannot become a physics result):**

- **Conservation is free and is not the question.** Finite volume conserves exactly (~1e-15) on *every* grid, worst included.
- **But a two-point flux is INCONSISTENT on a non-orthogonal mesh** — O(1) relative error that **grows** under refinement (order **−0.5** on every quad grid). ⇒ *conservation is a scheme property; **consistency** belongs to the scheme AND the grid together.* Exact conservation does not mean you are solving the right equation. (This supersedes the earlier "conservation is a scheme property / isotropy is a grid property" decomposition.)
- **The corner is a red herring.** Every cell has exactly 4 *edges* — the 24 defective ones included — and all three routers (8-neighbour MFD included) conserve to 1.000000000000 there. The **fan** is wrong *everywhere*, not at corners: 20.76% mean error vs 5.17% for gradient-projected edge flux, measured against a cone's exact answer.
- **Correcting the scheme** (project both centres onto the edge normal *through the mid-edge* — Joseph's carve — **and** widen the gradient stencil to a quadratic fit over the Moore neighbours) takes our own grid **9.2e-1 → 3.6e-4**, against the best hexagonal mesh's 2.2e-4, with **no change to `CellId`, the quadtree, the store, Hilbert locality, or the KRNG**.

Newly measured and *not in the literature we read*: equal-area is a property of the continuous **map** that the discrete cell does not inherit — you can have exactly-equal **areas** or great-circle **edges**, not both (5.0% / 9.9% gap).
- **⭑ The MFD fan: the error is a BIAS, not noise — and it does not converge away** (added 2026-07-12, report **§6a**, `grid_lab` §9a). §6's fan numbers were **two cells at one level (L5, ~313 km)**; erosion runs at **L19 (~19 m)**, and §6's figures were `|errors|`, which had already thrown away the sign that carries the answer. ### ⚠ TWO LEADS THE REPORT NEVER TESTED — because it audited coordinates, not physics (2026-07-13)

*These reopen the question rather than settling it. Both come out of `doc/theory/discretisation-and-information.md`.*

- **STAGGERING is not hexagons, and it survives.** Staggering is about **where you store the components** — scalars at centres, **fluxes at faces** — not about the cell's *shape*. Hexagons were ruled out for being unable to quadtree; **staggering is entirely compatible with a cube-sphere quadtree** (`CellId` unchanged, 1→4 unchanged). It adds **no points**; it *uses the same points better* — staggering **halves the effective spacing for the derivative, which moves the null (checkerboard) mode out of representable range**, buying a wider representable band for free. It is what every serious geophysical model does (Arakawa C-grid). ⚠ **And per the structure table it may be MANDATORY for the ocean/atmosphere nomos: collocated grids get vorticity and geostrophic balance wrong, and the failure looks plausible.**
- **[⊘ untested — a lead, NOT a proposal] Vertex-centred FV on a triangular primary mesh.** Cardiff Fig. 11: **the vertex-centred dual of a TRIANGULAR mesh is a HEXAGON.** Triangles **do** quadtree. So one could store data at triangle *vertices* (which **nest** across levels) while computing fluxes on **hexagonal dual control volumes**, which are **exactly orthogonal by primal–dual duality** (measured 0.00°, and it is a theorem). **The report tested icosa-tri *cell-centred* (worst corrected accuracy) and icosa-hex *Voronoi dual* (best accuracy, no quadtree) — it never tested the configuration that might have both.** ⚠ **The likely-fatal objection: the dual HEXAGONS do not nest** (a coarse hexagon is not the union of fine ones) even though the vertices do — which probably breaks the exact-nesting the seam needs. **Unknown. Must be measured before anyone believes it.**
