## ⛔ Prior v3 — the two-mode crustal prior. **PROMOTED TO A HARD COHERENCE REQUIREMENT (2026-07-12): as declared, this world cannot exist.**

**MEASURED (examples/sea_level_probe.rs):** the conserved ocean the `hydrosphere` derives (1.3735e9 km³, from the ante-mundane water-mass fraction) **does not fit the terrain the spine generates.** Basin capacity filled to the highest peak (6605 m) is 1.3619e9 km³ — **ratio 1.01×**. Derived sea level sits **23 m ABOVE the highest ground**: land fraction **0.0%**, a total water-world. The 33.4% land we build and erode exists ONLY because `SEA_LEVEL_M` is *decreed* (4000 m, arbitrary), not *derived*.

The margin is the tell — 1.01×, not 10×. The total relief (~3.7 km) is roughly the right *scale*; the **distribution** is wrong. fBm is **unimodal**: no deep basins to hold the water, no high platforms to stand above it. Earth holds the *same* ocean and still keeps 29% land only because its hypsometry is **bimodal** (abyssal plains ~−4.5 km, continental platforms ~+0.5 km) — and that bimodality is **isostasy**: two crust types of different density floating at different heights (light granitic continental rides high; dense basaltic oceanic sits low).

**So bimodality is not a fidelity upgrade — it is the physical precondition for land existing at all.** Order of work: (1) a **crust-type field** (continental vs oceanic) with **isostatic heights derived from density**, replacing the single-population fBm; (2) **sea level becomes a derived nomos** consuming the ocean stock + hypsometry — retiring `SEA_LEVEL_M`, the arbitrary datum that *also* drowned the fluvial test footprints for months; (3) re-run `sea_level_probe` and the early-continents survey check (target: land 2–15%). Everything below is downstream of this.
