# Declared violation is disclosure, not license

Making a limitation or conservation break **visible** (ledger, status banner, audit) does not make it **permitted**. Disclosure is how someone can *decide* to wait; it is not a default that waiting is fine. Conservation and other consistency laws of the architecture are not fidelity rungs that may be deferred because “it is declared.”

## Formal Expression

1. **Disclosure ≠ permission.** A declared deficiency, unmet flux, provisional root, or known mass leak is **honest** only if it remains a **live debt** someone can schedule, refuse, or fix. It is **not** a license to treat the violation as acceptable architecture until an arbitrary later phase.
2. **Consistency laws are not fidelity rungs.** Conservation (and peer consistency laws of the frame) are not “higher-fidelity later.” They are conditions under which the world remains a single coherent artifact. Scheduling their remediation “post-parity” because they are listed somewhere is the failure mode (paid-for session correction 2026-07-10).
3. **Ledger purpose.** Ledgers and audits exist so that *waiting is a decision*, not an ambient default. The reasoning shape to reject: “it is in the ledger, therefore it can wait.”
4. **Relation.** #norm-declaration-must-convict forbids unconvictable wishes. This norm forbids using *successful* declaration as absolution. #form-flux-web unmet edges and provisional admission are *visible* — not free.
