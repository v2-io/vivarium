# Probes before claims

No physics or world-behavior claim without a probe that can fail. Plausibility dressed as verification is a process failure.

## Formal Expression

1. **Probe requirement.** A claim about what the world does (flow, erosion, conservation, seam behavior, climate, land fraction, …) is not assertable as established until a probe exists that would fail if the claim were false.
2. **A probe that cannot fail is not a probe.** The physics must actually execute at the probe's footprint; a ratio of zeros, a no-op on submarine cells, or a tolerance sized to hide the defect is not evidence.
3. **Confirming numbers are suspect.** A result that matches prior expectation deserves more scrutiny than a surprise, not less.
4. **Rationalization is not diagnosis.** Explanations that preserve a preferred narrative (e.g. "erosion is invisible at macro scale") without checking the enabling condition (e.g. uplift zero, footprint underwater) are rejected when found.
